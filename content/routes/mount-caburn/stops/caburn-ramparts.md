# Crossing the Line

The path breaches the rampart at its north-east gap. Step onto the bank crest and the whole circuit reveals itself — a green ring pinched tight around the summit dome.

## Look Out For

- The counterscarp of the outer ditch, sharpest on the northern arc
- How the bank fades almost to nothing above the southern precipice, where the slope does the work
- Lewes Castle aligned in the valley gap to the west

> "One bank, one ditch, one hectare — and yet more digging went into this summit than almost any other in the south."

## Two Walls in One

Run your eye along the inner face of the bank. The subtle change in pitch halfway up is the join between the Iron Age dump and the later refacing — two societies, centuries apart, agreeing the line still mattered.
