# Interior Space

Standing inside the enclosure, you can appreciate the scale of this space. The interior covers approximately 24 hectares, making it one of the largest hillforts in southern England.

## What Excavations Revealed

Archaeological investigations have uncovered:

- **Neolithic flint mines** dating to c. 4000–3500 BCE, predating the hillfort
- **Iron Age occupation** evidence including pottery, metalwork, and hearths
- **Romano-British reuse** of parts of the site, suggesting continued significance

## Viewshed

From this position, you can see:
- The English Channel to the south (on clear days)
- Chanctonbury Ring to the west
- The South Downs escarpment

This strategic location suggests the site was chosen for its commanding views as well as its defensive potential.
