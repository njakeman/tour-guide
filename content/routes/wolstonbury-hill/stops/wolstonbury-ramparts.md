# The Wrong Way Round

Walk the circuit of the bank and look carefully at its cross-section. On nearly every hillfort in Sussex the ditch sits outside the rampart, so material dug from the ditch built the bank in front of it. Here the arrangement is inverted.

## Look Out For

- The ditch running along the inner foot of the bank
- The gap on the north-east side, thought to be an original entrance
- Cissbury Ring and Chanctonbury Ring on the western skyline — both later, both truly defensive

> "Wolstonbury asks a question the other hillforts don't: what if the point was never to keep anyone out?"

## Reading the Earthwork

The bank now stands barely a metre high, but section drawings from early excavations suggest it was once more than twice that, capped with chalk rubble that would have shone white across the Weald.

Continue clockwise along the bank towards the summit plateau.
