# Leaving the Weald

The climb begins in the trees at the foot of the scarp. Within a few minutes the beech canopy opens and you step out onto chalk grassland — one of the richest habitats in Britain, with up to forty plant species in a single square metre.

## Look Out For

- The deep hollowing of the path where it cuts the steepest slope
- Anthills of the yellow meadow ant, some over a century old
- Round-headed rampion, the "Pride of Sussex", flowering in late summer

> "A bostal is not built. It is worn — a negotiation between the hill and everyone who has ever climbed it."

## The Orchid Bank

Pause where the path bends east. The bank on your right is unimproved chalk turf, never ploughed or fertilised.
